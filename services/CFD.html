Computational Fluid Dynamics

  • HOME
  • SERVICES

A subfield of fluid science and technology known as computational fluid dynamics (CFD) simulates fluid flow, phase change, and temperature changes in any thermo-fluid system using cutting-edge numerical techniques and computer program-based algorithms.It is used to resolve or simulate the flow fields connected to any kind of fluid and forecast changes in the intrinsic and extrinsic characteristics of any closed system. The use of CFD has increased with the development of graphics processing units in computer engineering because it makes it easier to visualize the resolved fields of fluid property in more understandable forms.

To better comprehend different flows and related phenomena, you can use CFD to model complex situations involving fluid-fluid, fluid-solid, or fluid-gas interactions. The findings can then be analyzed.

A few of the research and engineering issues to which CFD is applied include Aerodynamics, Aerospace Analysis, Hypersonics, Weather Simulation, Natural Science & Environmental Engineering, Industrial System Design & Analysis, Fluid Flows & Heat Transfer, Engine & Combustion Analysis.

The major areas we work are listed as follows:

  • Fluid Structure Interaction (FSI)
  • Hydrodynamic analysis
  • Aeroacoustics
  • Combustion
  • Single phase and Multiphase flows

Computational Fluid Dynamics is a key research and service area for the Engineering Service practice. The Endlich team has extensive experience across multiple domains, helping design engineers to understand the critical areas where fluid-induced loads act, predict flow parameters, provide design countermeasures and thus realizing the product’s design goals

Our CFD Consulting Services findings apply to aerospace, automobile, oil and gas, HVAC, electronics, energy, marine, chemical, and medical sectors. It plays a pivotal role in achieving precision in aerospace, HVAC, and automotive industries, contributing to aerodynamics, energy efficiency, and product performance.
